What the story shows

  • David chooses safety over truth.
  • Giovanni is abandoned and falls into despair.
  • Hella is hurt by David’s dishonesty and leaves.
  • David is left alone with a guilt that cannot be torn up and thrown away.

Theme: Identity & Self-Expression. David’s fear of expressing his true identity damages his relationships and leaves him with guilt. Baldwin’s novel asks what it costs when we hide who we are in order to be accepted.

For the Teacher

This short interactive game retells James Baldwin’s Giovanni’s Room in five playable scenes and was built to meet the project requirements:

  • Characters: David, Giovanni, Hella, David’s father, Jacques, and Guillaume all appear and speak.
  • Major plot events: the frame night in the south of France; David’s childhood, the Joey memory and his father’s expectations; meeting Giovanni at Guillaume’s bar; life in Giovanni’s room; Hella’s return and David’s silence; Guillaume’s death and Giovanni’s sentence; Hella’s departure; David alone with guilt.
  • Central conflict: truth and self-expression versus social acceptance and safety.
  • Theme: Identity / Self-Expression.
  • Citations: ten collectible evidence cards, two per scene, each labeled as a paraphrase with Part/Chapter attribution. The citation field uses “Baldwin, p. ___” so the exact page can be filled from the physical class copy before submission.
  • Interactivity: first-person movement, examinable objects, evidence cards, and branching dialogue choices that return to the novel’s actual plot.

Note: for playtime the scenes condense a few events. Jacques’ warning is paraphrased in the bar scene, while the journal card identifies it as Part One, Chapter Three.
