What the story shows

  • David chooses safety over truth.
  • Giovanni is abandoned — and destroyed.
  • Hella is hurt most by David’s dishonesty, and leaves.
  • David is left alone with a guilt that cannot be torn up and thrown away.

Theme: Identity & Self-Expression. David’s fear of expressing his true identity destroys everyone he loves and leaves him with nothing but guilt. Baldwin’s novel asks what it costs — to ourselves and to the people who love us — when we hide who we are in order to be accepted.

For the Teacher

This short interactive game (5–10 minutes) retells James Baldwin’s Giovanni’s Room (1956) in five playable scenes, and was built to meet the project requirements:

  • Characters: David (the player), Giovanni, Hella, David’s father, Jacques, and Guillaume all appear and speak.
  • Major plot events: the frame night in the south of France; David’s childhood, the Joey memory and his father’s expectations; meeting Giovanni at Guillaume’s bar; life in Giovanni’s room; Hella’s return and David’s silence; Guillaume’s murder and Giovanni’s death sentence; Hella’s discovery and departure; David alone with his guilt.
  • Central conflict: truth (self-expression) versus social acceptance (safety). In the final scene the player may “try” honesty — the game then notes, respectfully, that this is not what David does in the novel, and follows the book.
  • Theme: Identity / Self-Expression — stated on the final summary screen.
  • Citations: ten collectible quote cards, two per scene, each labeled as a paraphrase (or a very short, well-known direct line) with accurate Part/Chapter attribution. Because page numbers vary between editions, every card states “Page number depends on edition” rather than inventing a number. Cards can be reviewed any time in the Quotes Journal (Esc menu).
  • Interactivity: first-person movement, examinable objects, and branching dialogue choices that always converge back to the novel’s actual plot.

Note: for playtime the scenes condense a few events — for example, Jacques’ famous advice, spoken the morning after the bar night in the novel (Part One, Chapter Three), is heard inside the bar scene.
